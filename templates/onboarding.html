Welcome to PhishGuard

PhishGuard analyzes suspicious emails to detect phishing attacks, malware, and social engineering attempts. Let's walk through how it works.

How Email Analysis Works

When you upload a suspicious email (.eml file), PhishGuard runs it through multiple detection layers:

🔎

Header Forensics

We check who really sent the email. Email headers contain authentication records called SPF, DKIM, and DMARC — these are like digital signatures that verify the sender's identity.

SPF = checks if the sending server is authorized by the domain
DKIM = verifies the email wasn't tampered with in transit
DMARC = the domain's policy on how to handle failed checks
🌐

URL Analysis

Every link in the email is safely inspected. We follow redirects, check for look-alike domains (like "g00gle.com"), and even render the destination page in a sandboxed browser to detect credential-stealing forms.

📄

Attachment Scanning

Attachments are hashed and checked against malware databases. We detect hidden macros, executable files disguised as documents, and match against YARA threat signatures.

🧠

AI Classification

A machine learning model and natural language analysis scan the email's wording for urgency pressure, threats, impersonation patterns, and social engineering techniques.

Understanding Your Results

Every analysis produces a threat score from 0 to 100. Here's what the levels mean:

CLEAN
Score 0-9 — No threats detected. The email looks legitimate.
LOW
Score 10-29 — Minor oddities found but likely harmless. Maybe a missing header or unusual formatting.
MEDIUM
Score 30-49 — Suspicious elements worth investigating. Could be a targeted phishing attempt.
HIGH
Score 50-69 — Strong phishing indicators. Don't click any links or open any attachments from this email.
CRITICAL
Score 70-100 — Almost certainly a phishing attack. Report it to your IT team immediately.
What's in the full report?
Each report shows a detailed breakdown of exactly what was flagged and why, mapped to MITRE ATT&CK techniques used by real threat actors. You can export results as PDF, JSON, STIX 2.1, or CSV.
